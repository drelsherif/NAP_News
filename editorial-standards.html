Editorial Standards

Neurology AI Pulse is a clinician-led publication designed to provide distilled, practice-relevant insights on AI in neurology, with a specific emphasis on governance, safety, and accountability.

Purpose and Scope

The Pulse highlights meaningful clinical innovation, emerging legal and regulatory developments, and practical guidance for navigating AI and generative AI in healthcare. We prioritize signal over volume, and clarity over hype.

Editorial Independence

Content is selected editorially. Inclusion does not constitute endorsement. The Pulse is not sponsored, and editorial choices are not driven by vendor relationships.

Selection Principles

  • Clinical relevance to neurology or neuroscience-adjacent practice
  • Impact on patient care, documentation, or workflow
  • Governance implications: safety, privacy, bias, accountability, consent
  • Actionable insight: clear takeaways and paths for deeper exploration

Evidence and Sources

We prefer primary sources (peer-reviewed studies, legal scholarship, regulatory texts, official guidance). News sources may be included when reporting verifiable clinical, legal, or regulatory developments. Commentary is clearly separated from cited content.

Clinical and Legal Boundaries

Neurology AI Pulse is educational and does not provide medical or legal advice. It does not replace clinical judgment, institutional policy, or professional consultation.

AI Use and Transparency

AI tools may assist with drafting, summarization, or language refinement, but editorial content is curated and finalized by a clinician-editor. AI output is not treated as authoritative without verification.

Corrections

If an error is identified, we aim to correct it promptly and update context as evidence and regulations evolve.

Editorial philosophy: AI in neurology should be powerful, transparent, and accountable—and always subordinate to human clinical judgment.
